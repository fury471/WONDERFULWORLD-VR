# Explore The Wonderland
## Current Demonstration Direction v1.0

- Date: April 14, 2026
- Status: active source-of-truth framing for the current demo and milestone docs
- Scope: design framing, play logic framing, interaction framing, art direction framing, and world-boundary strategy

## 1. Core Framing

The project is still an open-world-style VR fantasy, but the current demonstration should be framed as a **bounded, seamless-feeling magical amusement park** rather than an endless terrain or giant sandbox.

This is not a downgrade of the vision.

It is the correct production interpretation of the vision for the current team, timeline, and hardware constraints.

The demo should feel like:

- a handcrafted wonderland park
- multiple themed lands and attractions
- scenic connectors and sightlines between them
- soft discovery and free exploration
- a world that feels larger than the playable footprint

The demo should not depend on:

- infinite terrain
- very large empty traversal spaces
- forced mission structure
- strict attraction order
- obvious blocking walls that break the fantasy

## 2. Open-World Illusion Strategy

For the current project, open world means:

- the player can move through a meaningful, cohesive park slice in a mostly non-linear way
- the world feels continuous and explorable
- attractions can be discovered in flexible order
- the park suggests that more world exists beyond the immediately playable footprint

It does **not** mean:

- infinite map generation
- fully unbounded landmass
- endless traversal in every direction

The correct demonstration target is:

- a **complete-feeling exploratory park slice**
- built from authored spaces, attraction logic, scenic transitions, and soft perimeter design

## 3. Play Logic Direction

The demonstration should be **exploration-first**.

That means:

- no required play order by default
- no hard sequence gate that forces every player into the same attraction order
- onboarding should teach controls and then release the player into exploration
- systems should behave like discoverable attractions and environmental possibilities
- route references in older documents should be treated as a **suggested first-read orientation arc** for demoing, readability, and testing, not as a mandatory mission sequence

Good play logic examples:

- the player notices a scale-shift attraction and chooses to try it
- weather changes invite curiosity and make a land feel different
- growth reveals a path and rewards observation
- particle shaping and fireworks read as expressive attractions
- the cat ride works as a scenic connector, not just a required checkpoint

## 4. Interaction Design Direction

Interaction design should feel like a theme-park attraction layer over a free-roam park.

That means:

- interactions are discoverable
- interactions are readable from the environment
- interactions are staged clearly enough for first-time users
- interactions should be pleasant and theatrical, not hidden puzzle logic

Preferred interaction design rules:

- use strong landmarks and visible affordances
- use diegetic prompts, scenic framing, and proximity-based cues
- avoid dependence on debug-only or hidden knowledge in the integrated slice
- keep optional simulator/debug paths for development, but do not rely on them as the only readable play path

## 5. Boundary Design Direction

The current demonstration should use **soft boundaries** and **park perimeter illusions**.

Use boundaries that feel like believable parts of the park:

- hedges
- flower walls
- decorative fences
- water edges
- closed gates
- cliffs
- dense magical fog
- theatrical light walls
- distant skyline silhouettes
- decorative structures that imply more park beyond the playable slice

Do not use obvious hard walls unless they are strongly disguised.

The player should feel:

- “this park continues”
- not “the level ends here”

## 6. Art Direction Direction

Art direction should support the illusion of a complete magical park.

Priority goals:

- strong skyline silhouettes
- strong attraction sightlines
- color-coded lands with distinct identities
- theatrical staging and spectacle moments
- cohesive wonderland-park visual language

Art does not need to make the world bigger by raw size.
Art should make the world feel bigger by:

- layered depth
- framed vistas
- readable landmarks
- controlled reveals
- atmosphere and mood transitions

## 7. How To Read Older Docs

Any older document language that says:

- “route order”
- “park loop”
- “flow pass”
- “first attraction, second attraction, finale”

should now be interpreted as:

- a recommended first-read attraction arc for demoing and debugging
- a readability spine for early testing
- not a mandatory player path in the final demonstration

If a document conflicts with this direction, this file is the current framing source of truth.
